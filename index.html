2026 NATIONAL DISASTER RESILIENCE MONTH (NDRM)

National Disaster Resilience Month (NDRM) is an annual observance held in July in the Philippines, mandated by Executive Order No. 29, s. 2017. It shifts the national focus from mere disaster awareness to building active disaster resilience, emphasizing the capacity of individuals, communities, and institutions to adapt to, withstand, and recover from hazards.

Building a Disaster-Resilient Tabaco.

Who We Are

Tabaco City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO) is the lead city's agency responsible for steering local efforts in disaster response. Our mandate spans mitigating geographic hazards, preparing local communities, ensuring rapid tactical deployment during crises, and executing comprehensive post-crisis rehabilitation frameworks.

The Four Operational Pillars

1. Prevention & Mitigation

Conducting engineering controls, hazard mapping, and community risk assessments to eliminate or drastically minimize impact before threats manifest.

2. Disaster Preparedness

Equipping families, local business networks, and responders with actionable safety protocols, regular drill simulations, and early warning sirens.

3. Disaster Response

Deploying search and rescue assets, asset security frameworks, relief supply logistics, and immediate incident command protocols during live events.

4. Rehabilitation & Recovery

Structuring post-disaster infrastructure checks, medical recovery phases, psychological support, and rebuilding communities stronger than before.
